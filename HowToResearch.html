How To Research

Objective

The intended use case of this resource page is to provide tools to assist in research. IT compliance is a very deep and interesting area of expertise which requires a vast knowledge of both the technical and legal aspects of IT. This page is intended to provide you with tools on how to conduct useful research to further enhance your understanding of IT compliance.

Proper research involves utilizing the right tools. In this case, the tools would be in the form of websites and articles. To begin conducting research on IT compliance, you will first need a search engine of some sort. While Google.com will be the default, there are much better options to research with. The following are some useful alternatives to Google.com that will generate more accurate, useful, and better-filtered search results.

  • Google Scholar
  • Google Books
  • Campbell Library
  • Microsoft Academic
  • Research Gate
  • iSEEK Education
  • Virtual LRC
  • Academic Index
  • Digital Library of the Commons Repository
  • Library of Congress
  • Archives Hub
  • National Archives

You can click the above icons to navigate to the various research sites. They will navigate you to the appropriate pages.


Source Reliability

While there may be numerous search results, not all may be reliable. There are some general guidelines for determining the reliability of sources on the Internet. The first is to look at the domain type. The table below reviews the reliability of various types.


Domain Type Definition Reliability
.com Commercial Somewhat Reliable - This will depend heavily on the sources of information for the website
.edu Educational Highly Reliable - Most information on these sites comes from scholarly sources and can mostly be trusted for accuracy
.gov Government Highly Reliable - Most information comes from professionals in the US government and can usually be trusted (especially on topics related to political operations)
.org Organization Rarely Reliable - These are websites created by any organization (usually private). Accuracy of information from these sites will rely heavily on the credibility of the organization hosting the website
.net NETwork Domain Rarely Reliable - These sites usually consist of media sources. These should be avoided when conducting research
.mil Military Somewhat Reliable - These sites are hosted by the US government and can usually be trusted (especially for information regarding military operations)

Effective Searches

Now that we have a basic understanding of where we can conduct searches, we need to be able to create an effective search. The goal is to include strong keywords related to the intended search.

For example, if you are researching the diet of vampire bats, you might initially search "what do vampire bats eat?". A better, more focused search would be "vampire bat diet", "diet of vampire bats", or "common prey of vampire bats".

In the related topic of IT compliance, some general search queries for research might include "IT compliance regulations" or "IT compliance documentation", etc. There are endless possibilities for search queries to learn about this topic. In summary, ensure that the search string has strong keywords to populate useful and relevant sources.
