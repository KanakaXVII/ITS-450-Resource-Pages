Standards

Objective

IT compliance refers to compliance with something. This page is meant to explain the difference between those things that we are trying to be in compliance with. There are several different things that an organization is in compliance with such as:

  • Internal Controls
    • Organization Specific Goals
  • External Controls
    • Standards
    • Regulations
    • Legal Requirements

Compliance means to follow all requirements, both organizational and legal, to ensure that the organization is secure and certified to conduct business. Internal controls may be anything the organization deems necessary to conduct business securely. For example, this could include having encrypted hard-drives or maintaining password requirements. External controls refer to controls created by other organizations or the law that the organization must follow to conduct business securely.

There are many different organizations that have created compliance standards to follow, such as ISO and HIPAA. These standards are widely adopted and used by organizations around the globe. Below are links to some Compliance Standards that are commonly used.
